About Dottask

Dottask is a tool for quickly making directed graphs. I originally created it for personal use and so that I could try learning Reagent and Clojurescript. While it's still more of a pet project than a mature tool, I've already personally found it to be quite useful and fun. Please feel free to give it a try and tell me what you think on Twitter/GitHub.

Why I am making Dottask

I love to use graphs to help plan a new project or communicate concepts to others. They let me think visually and spatially about problems, while remaning structural enough to keep me focused on my goals.

Graphviz is my favorite tool for making graphs. I can create the nodes and edges via text entry or programmatically, and it automatically generates pretty great layouts. Thanks to viz.js, there are multiple web-based graphviz IDE's that let you instantly see graphs alongside their textual definition. However, having to edit this textual representation to change the graph can get a bit cumbersome, and I suspect that this is one of the reasons that more people don't use this fantastic tool.

When I needed to brainstorm or plan, I'd often catch myself drawing graphs on pencil and paper instead of my beloved Graphviz. Sure, this meant that I needed to lay things out myself and frequently reach for an eraser to make edits. However, not having to deal with the textual representation was still a huge reduction in cognitive overhead and completely changed how I thought.

Having seen the pros and cons of both of these approaches to graph-making, I wanted to make a tool that gave me the best of both worlds. Dottask uses Graphviz internally to provide the same great automated layout. However, instead of editing a text file, it lets you use a mouse, a touchscreen or vim-inspired keyboard shortcuts to work much more quickly.
